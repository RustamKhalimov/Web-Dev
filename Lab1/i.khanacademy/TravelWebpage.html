Mountains

Info about mountains

Mountains are natural elevations of the Earth's surface that rise prominently above their surroundings. They are typically formed through geological processes such as tectonic activity, volcanic eruptions, and erosion.

Type`s of mountains

  1. Tectonic Mountains: Formed when Earth's tectonic plates collide, causing the crust to fold and rise (e.g., the Himalayas).
  2. Volcanic Mountains: Created by volcanic activity, as magma rises and solidifies (e.g., Mount Fuji in Japan).
  3. Erosional Mountains: Formed by weathering and erosion wearing down surrounding rock, leaving harder materials (e.g., the Catskills in the U.S.).

Famous Mountains

  1. Mount Everest (8,849 m): The highest peak in the world, located in the Himalayas.
  2. K2 (8,611 m): The second-highest peak, known for its challenging climbs.
  3. Denali (6,190 m): The tallest mountain in North America, in Alaska.
  4. Mount Kilimanjaro (5,895 m): A dormant volcanic mountain in Tanzania, Africa.

Fun Facts

  • Around 25% of the Earth's land area is mountainous.
  • The Andes in South America is the longest mountain range, stretching over 7,000 km.
  • The Rocky Mountains in North America are home to many national parks.
