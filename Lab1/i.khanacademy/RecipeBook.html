Recipe #1

  • Time: 2–2,5 hours
  • Serves with kutchup, mayonnaise, sour cream
Ingredients Quantity
minced meat, potatoes, onions and dough There are never many mants

Step 1: Knead a stiff dough of flour, water and a pinch of salt. Leave it to "rest" for 20-30 minutes.

Step 2: Finely chop the meat and onion (in equal proportions), add salt, pepper and spices to taste.

Step 3: Roll out the dough into a thin layer (1-2 mm) and cut it into squares (approximately 10x10 cm).

Step 4: Put the filling on each square and pinch the edges (the traditional shape is with a "pigtail" on top).

Step 5: Lubricate the steamer or mantovark with oil so that the mantas do not stick. Steam for 40-45 minutes.

Step 6: Serve with melted butter, sour cream or sauce.

Source: Manty`s recipes website

Recipe #2

  • Time: 1.5-2 hours
  • Serves with salat named "Achuchuk" and with chicken or quail eggs
Ingredients Quantity
Rice, meat (lamb, beef or chicken), carrots, onions, vegetable oil, garlic, spices (cumin, barberry, salt, pepper). This is usually one cauldron.

Step 1: Cut the meat into small pieces, carrots into thin strips, and onions into half rings.

Step 2: Heat the oil in a cauldron and fry the onion until golden brown. Add the meat and fry until golden brown. Then add the carrots.

Step 3: Add spices, whole garlic cloves (without peeling them).

Step 4: Rinse the rice in several waters and spread it evenly over the meat. Fill with hot water (so that the water covers the rice by 1 cm), do not stir.

Step 5: Bring to a boil, reduce the heat and cook under a lid until the water is completely absorbed. Leave to brew for 10-15 minutes.

Step 6: Pilaf is served hot, mixed before serving.

Source: Plov`s recipes website

Recipe #3

  • Time: 3–4 hours
  • Serves with patato`s,carrot`s,sauce named "Tuzdik" and a bowl of broth.
Ingredients Quantity
Meat (lamb, beef or horse meat), dough (flour, water, egg, salt), onion, herbs. Depends on the number of person

Step 1:Boil the meat in plenty of water with the addition of salt, bay leaf, pepper. Simmer over low heat until tender (2-3 hours).

Step 2: Knead a stiff dough, let it rest for 20 minutes, then roll out and cut into rectangles or diamonds.

Step 3: Boil the dough lozenges in the meat broth for 3-5 minutes until tender.

Step 4: Cut the onion into rings and fry or boil in a small amount of broth.

Step 5: Place the dough in layers on a large platter, spread the meat on top, pour onion gravy and garnish with herbs.

Step 6: Beshbarmak is served with a bowl of broth.

Source: Beshbarmak`s recipes website
